Survival on the Moon

STEP 1 - Individual Ranking (10 minutes)

Instruction: "Welcome to Survival on the Moon! Imagine this: You are a member of a space crew originally scheduled to rendezvous with a mother ship on the lighted surface of the moon. However, due to mechanical difficulties, your ship was forced to land at a spot some 260 km from the rendezvous point. While your crew survived, much of your equipment was damaged during the rough landing.

Before you lies a critical mission: traverse 260 km of lunar terrain to reach the mother ship. Your survival depends on making the right choices with your limited resources. In front of you, you'll find a list of 15 items that survived the landing. Your first task is to rank these items from 1 to 15, where 1 is the most crucial for survival and 15 is the least important.

Work independently - don't discuss with others yet. Think carefully about the lunar environment: no atmosphere, extreme temperatures, difficult terrain. How would each item help you survive? Take your time, and we'll discuss your choices later."

STEP 2

Instruction: "Now that you've made your individual choices, let's form teams of five. First, calculate the average ranking for each item within your team. This ensures everyone's voice is heard. Next, as a team, you'll have the opportunity to adjust three items' rankings - choose wisely which ones you want to modify. Remember, you'll need to convince your teammates why certain items deserve to be moved up or down in ranking."

STEP 3

Instruction: "It's time to equip your team for the journey. Based on your rankings, I'll give each team their top three items automatically - these represent your highest-priority equipment. Then, you'll draw blindly: two items from ranks 4-6, and one item from ranks 7-10. These six items will be all you have for your journey across the lunar surface.

Remember to check your item cards carefully - some have limited uses, while others are reusable. Managing these resources wisely could mean the difference between survival and failure."

STEP 4

Instruction: "Let's talk about the reality of lunar travel. Your journey spans 260 kilometers, or about 200 miles. On Earth, walking with a heavy backpack, you might average 4 km/h. But how fast do you think you can move on the Moon? [Wait for responses]

While lunar gravity allows for longer bounds, your practical speed will still be around 4 km/h due to three main factors:

  • Uneven lunar terrain
  • Regular cooling stops for your spacesuit
  • Limited mobility in your pressurized suit

Loping for 8 hours daily, you'll cover 30-35 km per day. This means your journey will take approximately 8 days. Every 30 km, you'll face a crisis that requires specific equipment to resolve. Success means continuing at normal speed; failure means setbacks and delays.

Study your equipment cards - notice how some items have limited uses while others can be reused. This information will be crucial for your survival strategy."

EVENT CARDS (PART-1 ALERT CARDS)

OXYGEN SHORTAGE

ALERT: Your primary oxygen indicators flash red. During the last airlock cycle, your supply was lower than calculated. Without immediate supplemental oxygen, your team faces a critical shortage within minutes.

  • Required: Oxygen tanks
  • Success: You quickly connect the backup oxygen tanks, restoring normal supply levels.
  • Failure: One crew member succumbs to oxygen deprivation (dead). Others stay/return to the start point for one round to retrieve emergency oxygen supplies (each remaining members receive an oxygen tanks card).

"It's terrifying how fast it happens. One minute you're fine, the next... you're gasping in vacuum. Always double-check your O2." - Mission Log #184

SOLAR STORM

ALERT: Your radiation detector is screaming. A sudden solar flare has created intense radiation levels. You need immediate shelter for the next 4 hours until it passes.

  • Required: Parachute silk OR life raft
  • Success: You create a makeshift shelter and wait out the storm.
  • Failure: One piece of your equipment gets damaged by radiation (randomly discard 1 item).

"The sun on the Moon isn't your friend. When a flare hits, you have minutes to find cover." - Veteran Lunar Walker

FATIGUE CRISIS

ALERT: Your team is showing signs of severe exhaustion. The constant loping motion has drained your energy. Your suits' monitors show dangerous drops in blood sugar levels.

  • Required: Food concentrate or Milk
  • Success: The concentrated nutrients revive your team's energy (if you use milk, it will be completely consumed and you cannot use it any more next time you have a fatigue crisis).
  • Failure: Exhaustion forces an 8-hour rest period (only one person can move forward, and the rest of your team cannot move for one day).

"You never realize how hungry you can get until you're bouncing across the Moon in a 200-pound suit." - Mission Report #223

NAVIGATION CRISIS

ALERT: You've entered a crater field with identical features in all directions. The sun's glare is creating confusing shadows, and your sense of direction is completely lost.

  • Required: Stellar map
  • Success: Using star positions, you reorient and find the correct path.
  • Failure: You waste precious hours going in circles (return to the start point).

"Everything looks the same here - endless gray desert and black sky. Without the stars, you're just guessing." - Commander Sarah Chen

MEDICAL EMERGENCY

ALERT: A team member's suit has developed an abrasion from Moon dust. The constant movement has caused a serious cut that needs immediate attention.

  • Required: First aid kit OR nylon rope (for repairs)
  • Success: You treat the injury and reinforce the suit.
  • Failure: One injured stays and cannot move anymore (will die if teammates do not reach the mothership by the time life basic resources run out).

"Moon dust isn't like Earth dust. It's like tiny shards of glass, constantly wearing at your suit." - Medical Officer's Report

COOLING CRISIS

ALERT: Your suit's cooling system is struggling in the intense lunar day. Without additional coolant (water), your body temperature is rising to dangerous levels.

  • Required: Water (2L), or parachute silk, or raft
  • Success: You replenish the cooling system and regulate temperature.
  • Failure: Heat exhaustion sets in. Mission ends.

"In sunlight, your suit can become an oven. Water isn't just for drinking - it's keeping you from cooking alive." - Survival Training Manual

COMMUNICATION CRISIS

ALERT: Finally within theoretical radio range, but the mother ship isn't where expected. They've had to adjust position due to terrain issues.

  • Required: FM receiver OR signal flares
  • Success: You establish contact and get updated coordinates.
  • Failure: You miss the rendezvous point (return to previous checkpoint).

"The worst feeling is being so close but not knowing if you're heading toward an empty landing site." - Mission Audio Log

FINAL APPROACH

ALERT: Multiple systems are redlining after the long journey. You face:

  1. Oxygen system fluctuating (need: Oxygen tanks OR rope for repairs)
  2. Visual contact needed with ship (need: Stellar map OR flares OR FM receiver)
  3. Crew member collapse (need: Water OR first aid kit OR food)
  • Required: Items for at least 2 of 3 crises
  • Success: You overcome final challenges and reach safety.
  • Failure: So close, yet so far. Mission incomplete.

"The last kilometer can be the longest. Everything that can go wrong, will go wrong." - Mission Control Golden Rule

EVENT CARDS (PART-2 CHANCE CARDS)

LUNAR CREVASSE

ALERT: A deep fissure blocks your path - too wide to jump normally but too time-consuming to circumnavigate. The smooth walls drop into darkness.

Choice:

  1. Pistol Propulsion (only for one person):
    • Jump across (+3m per shot)
    • Boost raft speed (uses 5 rounds)
    • Emergency brake on landing (uses 1 round)
  2. Raft CO2 Propulsion (for the whole team):
    • Use raft's CO2 tank for thrust
    • Mark off one CO2 tank use
    • Carries entire team across
  • Required: Either .45 Caliber Pistols OR Self-Inflating Life Raft
  • Success: Either pistol recoil or CO2 thrust gets you across safely. Mark off used resources.
  • Failure: Without propulsion or safety equipment, must backtrack to find crossing point (lose one checkpoint).

ABANDONED CACHE

ALERT: A glint of metal catches your eye - partially buried equipment from a previous expedition emerges from the regolith.

  • Choice: Excavate cache (takes 1 hour)
  • Success: Randomly acquire one:
    • Water (2L)
    • Food concentrate
    • Nylon rope

"Sometimes the Moon gives gifts. Always mark what you leave behind." - Salvage Protocol #7

SMOOTH TERRAIN

"Nature's lunar highways - smooth as glass and twice as fast." - Navigator's Journal

"The dust cloud came out of nowhere. Like powdered glass in a black wind." - Surface Team Report

"The endless black sky can mess with your mind. Keep your crew talking." - Psychological Assessment #103

"Sometimes the quick path isn't the right path." - Veteran Navigator's Warning

"Height advantage comes at a price on the Moon." - Surface Operations Manual

  • Failure: Lose one checkpoint worth of time

"The code of space: never ignore a call for help." - Emergency Response Protocol

METEOR SHOWER

  • Required: Parachute silk OR life raft for shelter
